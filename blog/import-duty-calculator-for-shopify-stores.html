What Are Import Tariffs and Why Do They Matter for Shopify Stores?

Import tariffs (or duties) are taxes collected by a country's customs authorities on goods imported from another country. For Shopify store owners in the US, this means that products sourced from countries like China, Mexico, or India are subject to additional taxes upon arrival. These taxes can range from 10% to over 50% of the product's value, significantly impacting your profit margins.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) for Shopify Sellers

How do I find the HS codes for my products?

Harmonized System (HS) codes are international standards for classifying products. You can find them using the US International Trade Commission's HTS search tool. However, for most e-commerce products, a general category (e.g., "electronics," "textiles") is sufficient for an accurate estimate with TariffSnap.

Can I add tariff costs to my product prices in Shopify?

Yes, and you should! Once you know your true landed cost, you can adjust your retail prices in Shopify to ensure you maintain a healthy profit margin. Some Shopify apps, like Magical Product Fees, can also help you add surcharges at checkout.

What's the difference between a tariff and a duty?

The terms are often used interchangeably. A tariff is a tax on imported goods, while a duty is the amount of tax owed. For practical purposes, they refer to the same cost you need to pay.

How often do tariff rates change?

Tariff rates can change due to trade policies and international agreements. For example, US tariffs on Chinese goods have changed significantly in recent years. TariffSnap stays up-to-date with the latest rates, so you don't have to.
