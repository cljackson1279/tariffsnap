Understanding HTS Codes and How They Affect Your Tariff Rate (2026)

Published on January 13, 2026 | By Chris Jackson

For e-commerce sellers who import products, the Harmonized Tariff Schedule (HTS) code is one of the most important yet often misunderstood pieces of information. This code is the key to determining the tariff rate for your products, and getting it wrong can lead to costly delays, fines, and inaccurate cost calculations. This guide will demystify HTS codes and show you how they affect your tariff rates.

What is an HTS Code?

The Harmonized Tariff Schedule (HTS) code is a 10-digit classification system used by the United States to categorize imported goods. The first six digits are part of the internationally standardized Harmonized System (HS), while the last four digits are specific to the US. Every product that is imported into the US must be assigned an HTS code.

Think of it as a universal language for customs authorities. It tells them exactly what your product is, which allows them to apply the correct tariff rate and ensure that your goods are compliant with all regulations.

How HTS Codes Affect Your Tariff Rate

The HTS code is the single most important factor in determining the tariff rate for your product. Each HTS code is associated with a specific duty rate, which can vary based on the country of origin. An incorrect HTS code can lead to either overpaying or underpaying your duties. While overpaying hurts your bottom line, underpaying can lead to penalties and legal issues.

How to Find the HTS Code for Your Products

Finding the correct HTS code can be challenging, but there are several resources available:

  • US International Trade Commission (USITC) HTS Search Tool: The official HTS search tool is the most authoritative source for finding HTS codes. You can search by keyword to find the appropriate code for your product.
  • Your Supplier: Your supplier may already know the correct HTS code for your products, especially if they have experience exporting to the US.
  • Customs Broker: A licensed customs broker can help you classify your products correctly and ensure you are using the right HTS code.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What happens if I use the wrong HTS code?

Using the wrong HTS code can lead to your shipment being delayed or held by customs, as well as potential fines and penalties. It can also result in you paying the wrong amount of duty.

Do I need an HTS code for every product I import?

Yes, every product that is imported into the US must be classified with an HTS code.

Can the HTS code change for my product?

Yes, the HTS is updated periodically to reflect changes in technology and trade patterns. It's a good practice to review your HTS codes regularly to ensure they are still accurate.
