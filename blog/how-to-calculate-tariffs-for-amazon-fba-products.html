Why Amazon FBA Sellers Can't Ignore Tariffs

When you import products to sell on Amazon FBA, you are the importer of record. This means you are responsible for paying all import duties and tariffs. These costs are not included in Amazon's FBA fees and can easily wipe out your profit margins if not calculated correctly. With tariffs on goods from China and other countries ranging from 10% to over 50%, a miscalculation can be devastating.

The landed cost of your Amazon FBA product is the total cost to get it from your supplier to an Amazon fulfillment center. This includes the product cost, shipping, insurance, customs clearance, and import tariffs. Without knowing this number, you can't accurately price your product or forecast your profitability.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) for Amazon FBA Sellers

How do tariffs affect my Amazon FBA fees?

Tariffs do not directly affect your Amazon FBA fees. However, they increase your landed cost, which in turn reduces your net profit margin after FBA fees are deducted. You need to account for both to understand your true profitability.

Can I use Amazon's FBA calculator for tariffs?

No. Amazon's FBA Revenue Calculator is designed to estimate fulfillment fees, not import tariffs. You need a separate tool like TariffSnap to calculate your import costs.

What is the de minimis value and how does it affect me?

The de minimis value is the threshold below which no duties or taxes are collected. In the US, this is currently $800. However, this exemption is under review and may change. Also, it applies to the value of a single shipment, so it's not always applicable for FBA sellers importing in bulk.

Should I use a customs broker?

For large or complex shipments, a customs broker is highly recommended. They can help you navigate the complexities of customs clearance and ensure you're paying the correct amount of duty.
