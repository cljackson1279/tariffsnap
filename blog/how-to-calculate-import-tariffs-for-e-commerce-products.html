What is Landed Cost and Why is it So Important?

Landed cost is the total cost of a product once it has arrived at your warehouse or fulfillment center. It includes the original price of the product, transportation fees, insurance, and, crucially, import tariffs and duties. Many e-commerce sellers make the mistake of only considering the product and shipping costs, leading to a distorted view of their profit margins.

Without an accurate landed cost, you risk selling products at a loss, underpricing your goods and losing money, or overpricing them and losing sales. Calculating your landed cost accurately is the only way to make informed decisions about sourcing, pricing, and overall business strategy.

Step-by-Step Guide to Calculating Import Tariffs

Calculating import tariffs can seem daunting, but it can be broken down into a few manageable steps:

Step 1: Determine the Value of Your Goods

The first step is to determine the dutiable value of your goods. This is typically the Free On Board (FOB) price, which is the price you paid your supplier for the products. It does not include shipping or insurance costs.

Step 2: Find the Correct HS Code

The Harmonized System (HS) code is an international standard for classifying traded products. Each product is assigned an HS code, which determines the tariff rate. You can find the HS code for your products using the US International Trade Commission's HTS search tool. While this can be complex, for many e-commerce products, a general category can provide a good estimate.

Step 3: Identify the Country of Origin

The country of origin is where your products were manufactured, not where they were shipped from. The tariff rate can vary significantly depending on the country of origin due to trade agreements and policies.

Step 4: Calculate the Tariff

Once you have the dutiable value, HS code, and country of origin, you can calculate the tariff. The formula is:

For example, if you are importing $1,000 worth of goods from China with a 30% tariff rate, the tariff would be $1,000 Ã— 0.30 = $300.

Putting It All Together: Calculating Your Landed Cost

Once you have calculated the tariff, you can determine your total landed cost:

Landed Cost = Product Cost (FOB) + Shipping + Insurance + Tariffs & Duties

This is the true cost of your product, and the number you should use to calculate your profit margins and set your retail prices.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between FOB and CIF?

FOB (Free On Board) means the seller is responsible for the goods until they are loaded onto the shipping vessel. CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) means the seller is responsible for the goods until they reach the destination port. The dutiable value is typically based on the FOB price.

Do I have to pay tariffs on samples?

It depends. In the US, there is a de minimis value of $800, meaning that shipments valued at less than $800 are generally exempt from duties and taxes. However, this is not always the case, and the rules can be complex.

How can I lower my tariff costs?

One strategy is to source from countries that have free trade agreements with your country. Another is to ensure your products are classified with the correct HS code, as an incorrect code can lead to a higher tariff rate.
